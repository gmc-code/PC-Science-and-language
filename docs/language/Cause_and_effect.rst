===========================
Cause and Effect Patterns
===========================

.. list-table::
    :widths: 20 25 35 40
    :header-rows: 1

    * - Context
      - Best Forms
      - Matching Cause-Effect Structures
      - Features and Benefits

    * - Lab reports
      - Implied connection, verb forms
      - 1 (Implied A. B.)
        5a (nomA causes nomB)
        5b (nomA results in nomB)
      - Objective, concise

    * - News articles
      - Text connectives
      - 2a (As a result, B.)
        2b (Consequently, B.)
        2c (Therefore, B.)
      - Clear flow for general readers

    * - Everyday speech
      - “So”, “because”
      - 3a (A, so B.)
        3b (A; hence, B.)
        7a (B because A.)
        7b (B since A.)
      - Natural, conversational

    * - Academic essays
      - Prepositional phrases, noun phrases
      - 4a (Because A, B.)
        4b (Due to nomA, B.)
        4c (As a result of nomA, B.)
        6a (The result of nomA is nomB.)
        6b (The cause of nomB is nomA.)
        9a (The reason for nomB is nomA.)
        9b (The cause of nomB is nomA.)
      - Formal, structured

    * - Scientific explanations
      - Verb forms (“causes”, “results in”)
      - 5a (nomA causes nomB.)
        5b (nomA results in nomB.)
        8a (nomB results from nomA.)
      - Precise, mechanism‑focused

    * - Historical or analytical writing
      - “Results from”, “as a consequence of”
      - 8a (nomB results from nomA.)
        8b (nomB occurs as a consequence of nomA.)
      - Emphasises outcomes
