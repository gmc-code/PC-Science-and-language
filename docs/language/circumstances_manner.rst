==========================================
Manner Circumstances in Earth Science
==========================================


| In SFL, *Manner* circumstances tell us **how** a process happens.
| They can express:

- **Quality** (in what way?)
- **Means** (by what means?)
- **Comparison** (like what?)
- **Degree** (to what extent?)

The table below summarises these four types with common prepositions and Earth Science examples.

.. list-table:: Manner Circumstances (Quality, Means, Comparison, Degree)
    :header-rows: 1
    :widths: 20 25 20 35

    * - Manner subtype
      - Meaning
      - Common prepositions
      - Example (Earth Science)
    * - Quality
      - In what way the process happens
      - **with**, **in**
      - Lava flows **with** great force.
    * - Means
      - By what means the process happens
      - **by**, **with**, **from**
      - Earthquakes are measured **from** seismic readings.
    * - Comparison
      - How the process is like something else
      - **like**, **as**
      - Ash spreads **like** smoke in the air.
    * - Degree
      - To what extent the process happens
      - **to**, **with**
      - Plates move **with** very little speed.
