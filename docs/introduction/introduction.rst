====================================================
PC-Science-and-language introduction
====================================================

PC-Science-and-language is a resource for teachers and students to explore the relationship between science and language. Science practical reports are a common form of scientific writing that require students to communicate their findings effectively. This resource provides literacy support for students writing scientific reports.

The resources include explanations of different paragraph structures, such as PEEL and PRO, as well as examples of how to apply these structures in writing about various science topics. The aim is to help students develop their scientific literacy and communication skills by understanding the linguistic choices that make scientific writing effective.

It provides guidance on how to structure scientific writing, use appropriate language features, and create cohesive and coherent texts. It focuses on helping students learn how to write about science using the Systemic Functional Grammar (SFG) framework. By using this resource, students can improve their ability to communicate scientific ideas clearly and effectively, which is essential for success in science education and beyond.
