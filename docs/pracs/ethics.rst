Honesty
----------

    •    Act with integrity by reporting all findings truthfully
    •    Reporting all data truthfully, even if it does not support the hypothesis
    •    Do not leave out inconvenient data points to make results appear more favourable.
    •    Record observations at the time rather than writing them up later from memory.
    •    Record and report observations accurately rather than guessing.
    •    Admit when a measurement was taken incorrectly.
    •    Acknowledge limitations of the data (e.g. small sample, old data).
    •    Disclose any conflicts of interest (e.g. if the study is funded by an investor who may benefit from certain findings).
    •    Accurately disclose the methods used so that studies can be independently verified.

===========================
Multiple-Choice Questions
===========================

1. Which action best demonstrates honesty in scientific work?
   a. Adjusting results to match the hypothesis
   b. Reporting all data truthfully
   c. Ignoring unusual data points
   d. Rewriting notes to look neater

2. Why should observations be recorded immediately?
   a. Memory can distort details
   b. It saves time later
   c. It makes the report look longer
   d. It avoids using instruments

3. What should a scientist do if they discover a measurement error?
   a. Hide it to avoid delays
   b. Repeat the experiment without noting it
   c. Admit the mistake and document it
   d. Change the value to the expected one

4. Which situation requires disclosure of a conflict of interest?
   a. The study uses old equipment
   b. The researcher dislikes the topic
   c. The research is funded by a group that may benefit
   d. The sample size is small

5. Why is it important to accurately describe the methods used?
   a. So others can independently verify the study
   b. To make the report sound more complex
   c. To impress funding agencies
   d. To reduce the length of the results section
