================================================
Errors: Improvements
================================================

Improvements arespecific strategies to minimise the impact of each error.

----

Improvements for Equipment errors
-----------------------------------

**Instrument errors** occur  when a piece of equipment is faulty or not set up correctly

1. Regularly inspect and maintain equipment
2. Ensure all equipment is correctly set up before use
3. Calibrate instruments according to manufacturer guidelines
4. Replace faulty or worn equipment immediately
5. Train users in proper equipment handling and setup

**Reading errors** occur  when the measuring instrument does not have a fine enough scale to take an accurate reading

1. Use instruments with finer scales for better precision
2. Read measurements at eye level to reduce parallax
3. Take multiple readings and calculate an average
4. Avoid obstructing the scale markings while reading
5. Ensure adequate lighting when reading instruments

**Zero errors** occur  when an instrument is not reset to zero before a measurement is taken

1. Reset instruments to zero before each measurement
2. Calibrate instruments regularly
3. Check for mechanical drift or misalignment
4. Avoid moving instruments unnecessarily between measurements
5. Train users to verify zero position before starting

----

Improvements for Procedural errors
-----------------------------------------

**Personal errors** occur  when the researcher makes a mistake in carrying out the procedure

1. Follow the procedure carefully step by step
2. Double-check calculations and data entries
3. Control all relevant variables during the experiment
4. Practice the technique before actual measurements
5. Work systematically to avoid skipping steps

**Parallax errors** occur  when a scale or measurement tool is read from an inconsistent eye level or angle

1. Always read scales at eye level
2. Use instruments with mirrored or digital scales
3. Take multiple readings from consistent positions
4. Stabilise the measuring device to avoid movement
5. Use appropriate markers or pointers to improve accuracy

**Human errors** occur  when the researcher makes a mistake in recording or interpreting data

1. Record data immediately after measurement
2. Double-check entries and calculations
3. Use templates or tables to organise data
4. Minimise distractions during data collection
5. Review all results for inconsistencies before analysis

----

Improvements for Measurement errors
---------------------------------------

**Random errors** occur  when there are unpredictable variations in the measurement process

1. Repeat measurements to average out fluctuations
2. Control environmental conditions (temperature, light, vibration)
3. Use well-maintained and stable equipment
4. Minimise handling or movement during measurement
5. Avoid extreme or unpredictable conditions when possible

**Systematic errors** occur  when there is a consistent bias in the measurement process

1. Calibrate instruments using known standards
2. Use multiple instruments to cross-check measurements
3. Identify and remove consistent biases in the method
4. Maintain equipment properly and replace faulty components
5. Compare results with reference data or literature values
