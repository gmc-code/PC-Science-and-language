================================================
Measurement errors
================================================

Measurement errors are unpredictable or consistent variations in the measurement process.

----

Types of Measurement errors
-------------------------------

**Random errors** occur when there are unpredictable variations in the measurement process

- Fluctuations in room temperature affecting results
- Small changes in electrical supply causing inconsistent readings on a digital meter
- Tiny air currents affecting the movement of a balance pan

**Systematic errors** occur when there is a consistent bias in the measurement process

- A scale that always reads 5 grams too high
- A thermometer that consistently reads 2°C lower than the actual temperature
- A ruler with a worn or chipped end that shortens every measurement
- A pH probe that has not been calibrated and always reads slightly acidic
