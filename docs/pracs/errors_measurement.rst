================================================
Random and Systematic Errors
================================================

Random and Systematic Errors are variations in measurements that affect precision or accuracy.

**Random and Systematic Errors**
- Describe variation in measured results.

.. list-table::
    :header-rows: 1
    :widths: 25 75

    * - Error Type
      - Description

    * - Random error
      - Unpredictable fluctuations between repeated measurements that reduce precision.

    * - Systematic error
      - A consistent bias in one direction from the true value, affecting accuracy.


Types of Random and Systematic Errors
-------------------------------------

**Random errors** occur when there are unpredictable variations in the measurement process:

- Fluctuations in room temperature affecting results
- Small changes in electrical supply causing inconsistent readings on a digital meter
- Tiny air currents affecting the movement of a balance pan

**Systematic errors** occur when there is a consistent bias in the measurement process:

- A scale that always reads 5 grams too high
- A thermometer that consistently reads 2°C lower than the actual temperature
- A ruler with a worn or chipped end that shortens every measurement
- A pH probe that has not been calibrated and always reads slightly acidic

    1. Which of the following is an example of a **random error**?

        | a. A scale that always reads 5 g too high
        | b. A stopwatch that was not reset between trials
        | c. A thermometer stored in sunlight
        | d. Fluctuations in room temperature affecting results

    2. What is the defining feature of a **systematic error**?

        | a. It introduces a consistent bias into all measurements
        | b. It occurs randomly and unpredictably
        | c. It affects all digital instruments

    3. Which action helps reduce **random error**?

        | a. Resetting the instrument to zero before use
        | b. Repeating measurements and averaging the results
        | c. Ignoring outliers to simplify calculations
        | d. Using an instrument that is consistently biased

    4. Which situation best illustrates a **systematic error**?

        | a. A sudden gust of wind affecting a balance
        | b. A student misreading the scale once
        | c. A scale that always reads 5 grams too high
        | d. A ruler placed at an angle by mistake for a single measurement

    5. How can a scientist detect a **systematic error**?

        | a. By taking only one measurement
        | b. By comparing results with a known standard
        | c. By ignoring unexpected results
        | d. By using a coarse-scaled instrument
